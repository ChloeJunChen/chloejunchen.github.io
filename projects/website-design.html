Website Design for Perception

Understanding how curvature in website elements impacts perceptions of luxury and approachability.

Background

How do you translate the elegance of a luxury hotel into pixels on a screen? For high-end brands, the challenge is to craft a digital experience that doesn’t just look luxurious—it feels luxurious. This project delves into the subtle yet powerful role of design elements, like rounded corners and sharp fonts, in shaping user perceptions. Can these small details evoke warmth, exclusivity, or sophistication? Our research reveals how these design choices go beyond aesthetics to forge emotional connections with users.

Key Concepts

Before diving into the research, let’s explore some important terms and why they matter.

Corner Curvature

The shape of edges in design—rounded or sharp—carries inherent emotional and psychological associations. These shapes influence how users interpret a design's tone, from friendly and inviting to precise and authoritative.

Font Curvature

Typography is more than text; it’s a visual language. The curvature of fonts communicates subtle cues about approachability, elegance, and professionalism, shaping how users connect with the content.

Hedonic Value

A design’s ability to evoke pleasure, delight, and emotional engagement. Beyond utility, hedonic value enriches user experiences, transforming a simple interaction into a memorable one.

Readability

The ease with which users consume and process text on a website. Clear and accessible text doesn’t just inform; it ensures users stay engaged and feel understood.

Perceived Luxury

It’s about the impression a user gets—that a brand is premium and offers something special. For luxury brands, this perception is critical to communicating their value and maintaining their identity.

Hypotheses

Focused on nine hypotheses to explore the effects of design curvature on user experience and luxury perception. These hypotheses were developed through a combination of design principles, psychological theories, and prior research on aesthetics and user behavior.

Corners shape emotions

Rounded corners are expected to make websites feel softer, more inviting, and enjoyable, while sharp corners are believed to give a sense of sophistication and luxury. (H1, H2)

These ideas come from the way different shapes naturally evoke emotional responses—soft edges feel friendly, and sharp ones feel premium.

Fonts and usability

Rounded fonts are thought to make reading more enjoyable and easier on the eyes (H3, H4)

This stems from research showing how font shapes impact both how we read and how we feel about the content.

Bringing it all together

When a website is both enjoyable to use and easy to read, it’s more likely to feel luxurious. Rounded corners and fonts can work together to make a website feel friendly and high-end, while sharp corners add an extra touch of elegance. (H5 - H9)

These links highlight how emotional and practical satisfaction work together to shape perceptions of high-end brands.

Methods

Combined both quantitative and qualitative methods to explore how design curvature influences user emotions, readability, and luxury perception. From developing realistic webpage prototypes to collecting rich user feedback and analyzing data with statistical tools, each step was designed to uncover the impact of subtle design choices on user experience.

1. Mock Webpage Development

Four webpage prototypes were created in Figma, each varying two key design elements:

  • Corner curvature: Rounded or sharp corners.
  • Font curvature: Rounded or sharp fonts.

The prototypes maintained consistent layouts, content, and imagery, ensuring that only curvature differences were tested. These designs were modeled after luxury hotel websites to keep them realistic and relevant.

2. Data Collection

I recruited 182 participants through Prolific Academic, ensuring a diverse sample. Each participant interacted with all four prototypes in a randomized order and completed a survey measuring:

  • Hedonic value: How enjoyable and aesthetically pleasing the webpage felt.
  • Readability: How easy it was to read the text.
  • Perceived luxury: Whether the webpage conveyed a sense of exclusivity and sophistication.

To ensure data quality, the survey also included:

  • Manipulation Check to confirm participants noticed and understood the design variations.
  • Attention Check to ensure participants were engaged and responding thoughtfully (e.g., embed “Select ‘strongly agree’ for this statement”).

Participants further provided open-ended feedback about their preferences and impressions, offering qualitative insights.

3. Data Analysis

  • t-Tests: Compared the effects of rounded vs. sharp designs on user metrics.
  • ANOVA: Analyzed interaction effects between corner and font curvature.
  • Regression Analysis: Explored how enjoyment and readability influenced luxury perception.
  • Mediation Analysis: Tested whether enjoyment and readability connected design features to luxury impressions.

All quantitative analyses were conducted in R. Open-ended responses were thematically analyzed to add depth to the quantitative findings.

Key Results

The study revealed impactful findings that highlight how subtle design choices influence user experience and perceptions of luxury.

Rounded designs feel enjoyable

Rounded corners and fonts made the webpages more pleasant to use, creating a positive emotional response.

Sharp corners feel luxurious

Webpages with sharp corners were seen as more exclusive and sophisticated, reinforcing the perception of luxury.

Rounded fonts are easier to read

Rounded fonts made the text easier to read, helping users engage with content effortlessly.

Best combination

Combining sharp corners for a luxury feel with rounded fonts for readability offered the best balance for luxury websites.

Design Impacts

The findings from this study offer actionable guidance for designers seeking to create user-friendly and luxurious digital experiences.

Strategic Balance: Sharp Corners and Rounded Fonts

Sharp corners convey professionalism and sophistication, while rounded fonts add approachability and engagement.

Combine sharp corners for layout elements like navigation bars and containers with rounded fonts for text to balance luxury and warmth, aligning with the brand’s identity.

Enhanced Readability for Crucial Content

Rounded fonts improve readability and reduce cognitive load, especially for key information and calls to action (CTAs).

Use rounded fonts for CTAs like "Book Now" or "Learn More," product descriptions, and service details to ensure clarity and guide user behavior effectively.

Improved Navigation Through Font Design

Rounded fonts enhance navigation by improving readability and simplifying the browsing experience.

Apply rounded fonts to essential navigation elements, such as menus, dropdown lists, and breadcrumbs. Pair these with rounded corners on icons and picture boxes to create a cohesive visual theme and intuitive interface.

Hedonic Design for Storytelling

Rounded corners and fonts evoke positive emotions, enhancing the appeal of narrative content.

Incorporate rounded design elements into sections highlighting brand heritage, product craftsmanship, or storytelling visuals like photo galleries and interactive timelines to create an inviting and immersive user experience.

Broader Applications Beyond Hospitality

The principles of curvature design apply across luxury industries, from fashion to real estate, enhancing both digital and print media.

Use rounded fonts in advertising to evoke warmth and emotional connection, and employ sharp corners for headlines or dividers to maintain sophistication. In sectors like jewelry or automotive, strategically blend these elements to align with brand messaging and audience expectations.
